Community Feedback SMS Program

Kumbu Connect partners with community-based organizations (CBOs) to gather real-time feedback from the people they serve. This page describes how the program works, how to join, and the rights of every participant.

Program Details

Organization Kumbu Connect
Program Name Community Feedback SMS Program
SMS Sending Number (833) 632-7146
Opt-In Method Inbound SMS keyword — consumer-initiated
Message Types Community impact ratings, follow-up check-ins, optional feedback stories
Message Frequency Varies — typically 2–5 messages per feedback session

How to Join

Participation is always voluntary and always started by you:

  1. You receive a CBO keyword from a participating organization — for example, KIWA.
  2. You text that keyword to (833) 632-7146 from your mobile phone. This is the affirmative opt-in action.
  3. Kumbu Connect receives your message, matches it to an active CBO record, and creates your feedback profile with a consent timestamp.
  4. The feedback flow begins — you will be asked to rate how the CBO has helped your community on a scale of 1 to 10, with optional follow-up questions.

Consent is never assumed from a prior relationship. You must take the clear affirmative action of texting a supported keyword to begin receiving messages.

Privacy & Data Use

Kumbu Connect stores the following information when you join the program:

  • Your mobile phone number
  • The CBO keyword used to enroll
  • The date and time of your enrollment (consent timestamp)
  • Your responses during the feedback conversation
  • Your current subscription status (active or opted out)

This information is used solely to facilitate the community feedback program and to maintain records of informed consent. Your phone number is never sold or shared with third parties for commercial or marketing purposes.

You may request access to or deletion of your data at any time by contacting [Insert contact email].

Record-Keeping

Kumbu Connect maintains opt-in records for each subscriber, including phone number, enrollment keyword, consent timestamp, subscriber status, and message transcripts. These records document the affirmative keyword opt-in action taken by each participant.

Supporting evidence available on request includes program instruction screenshots, exported subscriber records, and stored message transcripts.
