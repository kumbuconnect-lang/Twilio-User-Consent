Proof of Consumer Consent for SMS Messaging

This page documents how Kumbu Connect collects and records consumer consent before sending SMS messages for its Community Feedback SMS Program.

This version reflects the actual product flow used in Kumbu Connect: a community member texts a valid CBO keyword such as KIWA to a designated Twilio number to begin the feedback sequence. Replace the few remaining operational placeholders before using this page for Twilio review.

How Consent Is Collected

  1. A community member is invited to participate in CBO feedback by texting the relevant CBO keyword, such as KIWA, to [Insert Twilio phone number].
  2. The consumer sends the keyword from their own mobile phone, which is treated as the affirmative opt-in action.
  3. Kumbu Connect only starts the feedback flow after receiving that inbound keyword and matching it to an enabled CBO record.
  4. The application stores the subscriber phone number, the keyword used to enroll, and the consent timestamp when the enrollment is created.
  5. The subscriber can stop messages at any time by replying STOP and can request instructions by replying HELP.

Consent is not inferred from a prior relationship. The consumer must take the clear affirmative action of texting a supported keyword to begin the SMS flow.

Opt-In Instructions Provided to Consumers

The program instructions provided to consumers are substantively as follows:

To join Kumbu Connect community feedback messages for a participating CBO, text that CBO's keyword to [Insert Twilio phone number]. By texting the keyword, you agree to receive SMS messages about community feedback and follow-up check-ins. Message frequency varies. Message and data rates may apply. Reply STOP to opt out. Reply HELP for help.

Example Initial Program Message

After the keyword is received, the system begins the feedback flow with a message like this:

Thank you for joining [CBO Name] feedback. Reply with a number from 1 to 10 to rate how KIWA has helped your community.

Opt-Out and Help Language

  • Consumers can reply STOP at any time to stop receiving SMS messages.
  • Consumers can reply HELP for assistance.
  • When a consumer replies STOP, the application marks the subscriber as opted out and ends the active SMS conversation state.
  • Support contact: [Insert support email or phone number].

Recordkeeping

Kumbu Connect retains records of SMS opt-ins, including the subscriber phone number, the signup keyword used, the consent timestamp, subscriber status, and the transcript of the feedback conversation.

Supporting evidence that can be provided separately if requested includes screenshots of program instructions, exported subscriber records, and stored message transcripts showing the affirmative keyword opt-in.
