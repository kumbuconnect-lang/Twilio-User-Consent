How Consent Is Collected

  1. The consumer visits [signup page URL].
  2. The consumer enters their mobile phone number into the signup form.
  3. The consumer must actively check an unchecked consent box before submission.
  4. The form displays the SMS disclosure shown below at the point of collection.
  5. The consent record is stored with the submitted form data, including timestamp and phone number.

Consent is not inferred from a prior relationship. The consumer must take a clear affirmative action to opt in.

Consent Language Presented to the Consumer

The following disclosure is shown to the consumer before they opt in:

By providing your phone number and checking this box, you agree to receive SMS messages from [Organization Name] about [Program Name]. Message frequency may vary. Message and data rates may apply. Reply STOP to opt out. Reply HELP for help. Consent is not a condition of purchase or participation.

Example Confirmation Message

After opting in, the consumer may receive a confirmation message similar to the following:
